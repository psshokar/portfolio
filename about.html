About Me

As a child, I loved sports and always found my greatest joy in playing a game of basketball with my teammates.
My parents and siblings attended my games, cheering me on every step of the way for the 11 years I played on a team.
Spending time outdoors was always a top priority for me, so I enjoyed my after-school hours on the trampoline, jungle gym, and shooting baskets on the driveway.

In school, I enjoyed spending time with my friends, playing outside during recess, and learning from my kind-hearted teachers.
I attended a K-8 charter school where I began as a five-year-old Kindergarten student.
The uniforms, small class sizes, and strict discipline gave me a work ethic that has stuck with me throughout the years.
When I moved to my local public high school, I experienced a huge change, as the class sizes suddenly spiked to 35 students in each class and daily fights in the hallways.
It was eye-opening and challenging to continue to learn and grow in an environment so different from what I was used to.

After graduating from high school, I took a gap year and moved to Hawai‘i Island to build an updated technology infrastructure at a Title I public school.
I worked with teachers to prepare their classrooms for TVs and Chromebooks, I installed AP testing software on the library computers, and I managed the school’s upgrade in their overall use of technology tools.
The experience was insightful and humbling. I learned that so much of our own country, particularly in low-income areas, lacks access to the Internet and basic technology resources.
This pushed my desire to study Information Analysis and User Experience design at the University of Michigan.
My goal is to help empower communities through access to technology.
With access to information, community leaders all across our nation and globe will be able to change and lead their own communities for the better.
